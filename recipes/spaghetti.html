Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a type of long, thin pasta that originated in Italy. It is made from durum wheat semolina and water, and is traditionally served with a variety of sauces, such as marinara, Bolognese, or pesto. Spaghetti is a popular dish around the world and is known for its versatility, quick cooking time, and ability to pair with many different flavors.
